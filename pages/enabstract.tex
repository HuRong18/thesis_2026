% English Abstract

In recent years, with the rapid development of Internet of Things (IoT) and artificial intelligence technologies, emerging applications such as virtual reality, augmented reality, and autonomous driving have posed significant challenges to the computational capabilities at the network edge. Edge computing effectively reduces response latency by offloading computational tasks to edge nodes. However, with the surge of application data and the expansion of computational task scales, a single node can hardly support large-scale computational tasks independently, giving rise to distributed edge computing. Distributed edge computing aggregates computational resources from multiple edge nodes, decomposing large-scale tasks into multiple subtasks for parallel processing, thereby improving computational efficiency. Nevertheless, due to the heterogeneity in hardware configurations, load states, and network environments of edge nodes, some nodes may become stragglers due to resource competition or sudden failures, with computational speeds far behind other nodes, causing the overall computational latency to be limited by the slowest node. To mitigate the straggler effect, coded edge computing has been proposed as an emerging paradigm integrating erasure coding theory with distributed computing. By introducing redundancy in the task distribution phase, the central node only needs to collect computation results from a subset of faster nodes to reconstruct the original task results. However, most existing research on coded edge computing focuses on computational latency optimization, overlooking the communication latency that may arise from channel fading during the downlink transmission phase. In fact, the redundancy introduced by coded computing during task distribution creates strong correlation among computation results from different edge nodes, which offers potential for optimizing downlink transmission using distributed source coding techniques. Addressing these issues, this thesis investigates the joint optimization of coded computation result transmission mechanisms and distributed source coding from the perspective of distributed source coding, targeting two scenarios: complete recovery and linear recovery. The main research contents and contributions are as follows:

(1) Research on DSC-assisted coded computation result transmission method for complete recovery. For scenarios requiring complete reconstruction of all subtask computation results, a DSC scheme based on LDPC code syndrome puncturing is proposed. This scheme leverages the linear correlation among coded computation results and combines the Slepian-Wolf theorem to characterize the theoretical compression region for achieving complete recovery of computation results without communication. A coding scheme based on syndrome puncturing is designed, enabling edge nodes to adaptively adjust compression rates according to channel states, achieving flexible reallocation of transmission loads among nodes. A downlink transmission latency minimization model is established, and for this non-convex optimization problem, a joint optimization algorithm based on Convex-Concave Procedure (CCCP) is designed to jointly optimize source compression rates and transmit power of each edge node. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed scheme significantly reduces transmission latency in heterogeneous channel condition scenarios and exhibits good robustness to variations in the number of straggler nodes.

(2) Research on DSC-assisted coded computation result transmission method for linear recovery. For scenarios requiring only the recovery of linear aggregation values of computation results (such as gradient aggregation in distributed machine learning), a coding scheme based on nested linear codes is proposed. It is proved that under specific conditions, the achievable rate region for linear recovery is strictly larger than the classical Slepian-Wolf region, thereby achieving higher compression efficiency than complete recovery. A simplified form of the achievable rate region is provided, namely the union of the SW boundary and the linear recovery boundary, which reduces the complexity of solving the optimization problem. A corresponding latency minimization model is established, and an optimization algorithm based on CCCP is designed. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed scheme outperforms both independent coding schemes and SW coding schemes under heterogeneous and identical channel conditions, with more pronounced advantages in scenarios with many straggler nodes.

In summary, by exploiting the intrinsic correlation among tasks in coded edge computing, this thesis deeply integrates distributed source coding with coded computing, providing a new perspective and effective solutions for addressing communication bottlenecks in edge computing systems.
